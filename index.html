Banff / Canmore — Larch Season
Oct 1 (Tue) — Arrival
Land 17:30 MDT · collect car · drive Canmore/Banff ~1.5 hrs · arrive ~19:30. Dinner in Banff: Saltlik Steakhouse or Park Distillery. Early night — big days ahead.

Oct 2 (Wed) — Icefields Parkway Sampler
Drive north on Hwy 93 toward Lake Louise. Stop at Bow Lake (mirror reflection, easy 20-min walk), Peyto Lake lookout (10-min walk, unmissable), and Mistaya Canyon. Return through Banff townsite — wander Banff Ave, Johnston Canyon afternoon walk. Banff Gondola at sunset for Bow Valley panorama.

Oct 3 (Thu) — Lake Louise
Early start at Lake Louise (7am to beat crowds, golden morning light). Hike Plain of Six Glaciers trail (14km round trip, passes teahouse open mid-June to mid-Oct). Or shorter: Lake Agnes Tea House trail (7km). Afternoon: Fairview Lookout for aerial Lake Louise view. Drive back via Johnston Canyon lower falls (30-min return).

Oct 4 (Fri) — 🌟 Moraine Lake & Larch Valley
Moraine Lake shuttle confirmed — INPC26-13891950B1 · 7am slot
6:30am: Drive to Lake Louise shuttle hub. 7am: Bus to Moraine Lake. Hike Larch Valley trail (5.8km to valley, +2km to Sentinel Pass at 2611m). Peak golden larch color in early October — electric yellow against blue rock and snow. Best light: morning before noon. Return shuttle afternoon. Evening: Upper Hot Springs ($10pp) to rest sore legs.

🏔 Itinerary
Jasper — Icefields Parkway & Dark Skies
Oct 5 (Sat) — Icefields Parkway Drive
Depart Banff/Lake Louise 9am. Drive Hwy 93N (the Icefields Parkway — often called the world's most scenic drive). Stops:
· Crowfoot Glacier — quick pullout, dramatic hanging glacier
· Bow Lake — if not visited Oct 2
· Parker Ridge (optional 5.6km return) — views down the Saskatchewan Glacier tongue
· Columbia Icefield / Athabasca Glacier — walk to the toe of the glacier on foot. One of the few accessible glaciers in the world. Skywalk glass floor walkway nearby.
· Athabasca Falls — powerful canyon waterfall, 30-min stop
Arrive Jasper town ~5–6pm. Dinner: Treeline Restaurant or Sunhouse Café.

Oct 6 (Sun) — Full Jasper Day
Morning: Maligne Lake (45 min from Jasper). Spirit Island boat tour if running (typically Oct 11 is last day — check ahead). Or hike the lake's eastern shore (Mary Schaffer Loop, 3.2km). Midday: Maligne Canyon — 48m deep limestone slot canyon, walkway bridges across. Afternoon: Valley of the Five Lakes (4.4km easy loop, turquoise glacial lakes with fall color reflections). Evening: Jasper is a UNESCO Dark Sky Preserve — clearest skies in North America. Milky Way visible on clear October nights after 9pm.

Oct 7 (Mon) — Morning hike, drive to YYC
7am: Pyramid Lake (10-min drive from Jasper) — elk and mountain reflection at dawn. Quick hike: Old Fort Point (3.5km, panoramic Athabasca Valley views). Depart Jasper by 11:30am → drive Hwy 16 → Hwy 2 to Calgary (~3.5 hrs). Drop car at YYC airport by 3:30pm · Priority Pass lounge at YYC (CSR) · Evening flight ~21:00 MDT.
Maligne Lake Spirit Island boat tour ends ~Oct 11 — book online as soon as possible if you want it Oct 6. Jasper is colder than Banff (higher elevation) — pack a down layer. Elk rut peaks mid-Sept–Oct, common in Jasper townsite at dawn.

Japan — Should You Add It?

The Case For
Early October in Tokyo is near-perfect — low 20s, clear skies, first hints of fall color before peak foliage crowds. 2–3 nights slots naturally after Banff (Oct 5–7) before arriving Bangkok Oct 8, wedding Oct 9. The routing YYC → NRT → BKK is a clean westward arc.

Bangkok shrinks from 10 to ~7 nights. Another hotel booking and flight segment to arrange. Japan rewards a full week — 3 days is a taste, not a stay. Adds ~$150–300 in award fees and one more hotel to book during already busy larch season planning.
